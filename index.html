TOPIC 1: CONTEXT
WHEN IS ATTENDANCE TAKEN?

Attendance is taken multiple times during the course of a campaign execution. Mainly it is done at the time of:

  • Trainings
  • Registration
  • Distribution
WHY IS ATTENDANCE IMPORTANT?

The employees who are working for the campaign are typically paid based on the days worked; this information is taken from the attendance system.

WHAT HCM PROVIDES

HCM provides a feature to mark the present and absent for every employee present in a campaign using the mobile app. To enable this, the attendance module has to be configured appropriately.

KEY PREREQUISITE

Employee to campaign mapping is one of the key prerequisite information that needs to be mapped before one can use the module. Here in this module we will cover the mappings required for attendance.

CONVERTING DATES TO EPOCH TIMESTAMP

Dates must be in Epoch milliseconds format (13 digits). There are several ways to convert dates:

METHOD 1: ONLINE CONVERTER

Use an online converter:

https://www.epochconverter.com/

Enter your date, and copy the milliseconds value (13 digits).

METHOD 2: MANUAL CALCULATION

Epoch time is the number of milliseconds since January 1, 1970 00:00:00 UTC.

1

Calculate days from Jan 1, 1970 to your date

Example: Jan 1, 2026 = 56 years = approximately 20,454 days

2

Convert to milliseconds

Days x 24 x 60 x 60 x 1000 = milliseconds

20,454 x 86,400,000 = 1,767,225,600,000

METHOD 3: EXCEL FORMULA (RECOMMENDED)

Use this Excel formula to convert a date in cell A1 to Epoch milliseconds:

=(A1-DATE(1970,1,1))*86400000
WHY 86,400,000?

This number represents the number of milliseconds in one day:

CALCULATION VALUE
Hours in a day 24
Minutes in an hour 60
Seconds in a minute 60
Milliseconds in a second 1,000
24 x 60 x 60 x 1000 = 86,400,000 milliseconds

The Excel formula (A1-DATE(1970,1,1)) gives the number of days since January 1, 1970. Multiplying by 86,400,000 converts those days into milliseconds.

Step-by-step in Excel:

1

Enter your date in column A

Example: Cell A1 = 1/1/2026

2

Enter the formula in column B

Cell B1 = =(A1-DATE(1970,1,1))*86400000

3

Format as Number (no decimals)

Right-click cell B1 > Format Cells > Number > 0 decimal places

4

Copy the value

Result: 1767225600000
